Fertilization?

Fertilization is not necessary for when the bamboo plant reproduces using rhiozomes, because it is a form of asexual reproduction.

However, fertilization will occur when a bamboo plant flowers. Male gametes will be transferred to the female ovules, and this process is called pollination. Bamboo plants usually use wind pollination, which means that bamboo places do not need to spend resources on creating nectar, scent, or bright-looking flowers to attract pollinators such as bees. Instead, they create dry pollen which can be carried by the wind to other bamboo plants. Bamboo uses internal pollination for this process, as the pollen must go inside the flower. After pollination happens, the ovules will grow into seeds inside a fruit.
