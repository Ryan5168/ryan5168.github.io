Final Thoughts

I think the reproductive strategy of bamboo is very advantageous to the bamboo itself. Out of the two ways of reproduction for bamboo, vegetative propagation is better compared to flowering. Vegetative propagation allows bamboo to reproduce quickly and efficiently. This is shown by the literal forests of bamboo that grow throughout Asia. The bamboo plant's flowering may be inefficient, but it has vegetative propagation to serve as the main form of reproduction.

Something interesting I read about bamboo flowering is the reasoning behind the flowering cycle (every bamboo plant from the same species will flower at the same time period). If the bamboo plant produces a lot of seeds, then there will still be seeds left over after the animals of the forest eat their fill. However, it will take a while for all the seeds to grow back into bamboo plants, and after the bamboo plants seed, they will die. I think that this is a built-in defense mechanism for the bamboo to repopulate, which can be very helpful in their survival. The period between the death of the bamboo plants and the growing of the new bamboo plants could damage the humans and animals who depend on bamboo for their survival, so bamboo flowering is not the best type of reproduction.

To conclude, I believe that the bamboo's form of vegetative propagation is superior to the flowering method, because it takes less time and is less complex. Without vegetative propagation, bamboo would have a lot of trouble surviving if it only flowered every hundred years.
